
# Title 46 - Shipping
## Chapter I - Coast Guard, Department of Homeland Security
### Subchapter E - Load Lines
#### PART 42 - DOMESTIC AND FOREIGN VOYAGES BY SEA
##### Subpart 42.30 - Zones, Areas, and Seasonal Periods
###### ยง 42.30-15 Tropical Zone.
####### Southern boundary of the Tropical Zone.

(b)The southern boundary of the Tropical Zone is the rhumb line from the Port of Santos, Brazil, to the point where the meridian of longitude 40&#xB0; W. intersects the Tropic of Capricorn; thence the Tropic of Capricorn to the west coast of Africa; from the east coast of Africa the parallel of latitude 20&#xB0; S. to the west coast of Madagascar; thence the west and north coasts of Madagascar to longitude 50&#xB0; E.; thence the meridian of longitude 50&#xB0; E. to latitude 10&#xB0; S.; thence the parallel of latitude 10&#xB0; S. to longitude 98&#xB0; E.; thence the rhumb line to Port Darwin, Australia; thence the coasts of Australia and Wessel Island eastwards to Cape Wessel; thence the parallel of latitude 11&#xB0; S. to the west side of Cape York; from the east side of Cape York the parallel of latitude 11&#xB0; S. to longitude 150&#xB0; W.; thence the rhumb line to the point latitude 26&#xB0; S., longitude 75&#xB0; W.; and thence the rhumb line to the west coast of the American continent at latitude 30&#xB0; S.

(1) Coquimbo and Santos are to be considered as being on the boundary line of the Tropical and Summer Zones.
