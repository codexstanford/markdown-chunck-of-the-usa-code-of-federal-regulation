
# Title 21 - Food and Drugs
## Chapter I - Food and Drug Administration, Department of Health and Human Services
### Subchapter B - Food for Human Consumption
#### PART 112 - STANDARDS FOR THE GROWING, HARVESTING, PACKING, AND HOLDING OF PRODUCE FOR HUMAN CONSUMPTION
##### Subpart F - Biological Soil Amendments of Animal Origin and Human Waste
###### ยง 112.54 What treatment processes are acceptable for a biological soil amendment of animal origin that I apply in the growing of covered produce?
####### i.e.,

(1) Static composting that maintains aerobic (oxygenated) conditions at a minimum of 131 &#xB0;F (55 &#xB0;C) for 3 consecutive days and is followed by adequate curing; and

(2) Turned composting that maintains aerobic conditions at a minimum of 131 &#xB0;F (55 &#xB0;C) for 15 days (which do not have to be consecutive), with a minimum of five turnings, and is followed by adequate curing.
