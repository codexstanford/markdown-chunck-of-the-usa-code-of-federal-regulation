(a)serological reagents are devices that are used in the agglutination (an antigen-antibody clumping reaction) test to identifyfrom cultured isolates derived from clinical specimens. The identification aids in the diagnosis of cholera caused by the bacteriumand provides epidemiological information on cholera. Cholera is an acute infectious disease characterized by severe diarrhea with extreme fluid and electrolyte (salts) depletion, and by vomiting, muscle cramps, and prostration. If untreated, the severe dehydration may lead to shock, renal failure, cardiovascular collapse, and death.
