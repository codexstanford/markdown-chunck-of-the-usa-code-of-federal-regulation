
# Title 21 - Food and Drugs
## Chapter I - Food and Drug Administration, Department of Health and Human Services
### Subchapter C - Drugs: General
#### PART 201 - LABELING
##### Subpart G - Specific Labeling Requirements for Specific Drug Products
###### ยง 201.327 Over-the-counter sunscreen drug products; required labeling based on effectiveness testing.
####### UV exposure,Definition of minimal erythema dose (MED).

(5)-(i)The minimal erythema dose (MED) is the smallest UV dose that produces perceptible redness of the skin (erythema) with clearly defined borders at 16 to 24 hours after UV exposure. The MED for unprotected skin (MED) is determined on a test site that does not have sunscreen applied. The MED for protected skin (MED) is determined on a test site that has sunscreen applied. An MEDis determined for the SPF standard (ssMED). An MEDis determined for the sunscreen test product (tpMED).
