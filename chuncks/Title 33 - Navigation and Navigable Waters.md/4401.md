######## Nearest land.

The term "from the nearest land" means from the baseline from which the territorial sea of the territory in question is established in accordance with international law, except that, for the purposes of these regulations, "from the nearest land" off the northeastern coast of Australia shall mean from a line drawn from a point on the coast of Australia in-latitude 11&#xB0;00&#x2032; South, longitude 142&#xB0;08&#x2032; East to a point in-latitude 10&#xB0;35&#x2032; South, longitude 141&#xB0;55&#x2032; East, thence to a point-latitude 10&#xB0;00&#x2032; South, longitude 142&#xB0;00&#x2032; East, thence to a point-latitude 9&#xB0;10&#x2032; South, longitude 143&#xB0;52&#x2032; East, thence to a point-latitude 9&#xB0;00&#x2032; South, longitude 144&#xB0;30&#x2032; East, thence to a point-latitude 10&#xB0;41&#x2032; South, longitude 145&#xB0;00&#x2032; East, thence to a point-latitude 13&#xB0;00&#x2032; South, longitude 145&#xB0;00&#x2032; East, thence to a point-latitude 15&#xB0;00&#x2032; South, longitude 146&#xB0;00&#x2032; East, thence to a point-latitude 17&#xB0;30&#x2032; South, longitude 147&#xB0;00&#x2032; East, thence to a point-latitude 21&#xB0;00&#x2032; South, longitude 152&#xB0;55&#x2032; East, thence to a point on the coast of Australia in latitude 24&#xB0;42&#x2032; South, longitude 153&#xB0;15&#x2032; East.
