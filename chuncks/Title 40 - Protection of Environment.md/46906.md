###### § 86.144-94 Calculations; exhaust emissions.
####### For gasoline-fuel and diesel-fuel;

(A)Density= 16.33 g/ft-carbon atom (0.5768 kg/m-carbon atom), assuming an average carbon to hydrogen ratio of 1:1.85 at 68 &#xB0;F (20 &#xB0;C) and 760 mm Hg (101.3 kPa) pressure.

(B) For natural gas and liquefied petroleum gas fuel; Density= 1.1771(12.011 + H/C(1.008))g/ft-carbon atom (0.04157(12.011 + H/C(1.008))kg/m-carbon atom), where H/C is the hydrogen to carbon ratio of the non-methane hydrocarbon components of the test fuel, at 68 &#xB0;F (20 &#xB0;C) and 760 mm Hg (101.3 kPa) pressure.

(iii)(A) CH= Methane concentration of the dilute exhaust sample corrected for background, in ppm carbon equivalent.

(B) CH= CH- CH(1 - 1/DF)

(iv) CH= Methane exhaust bag concentration in ppm carbon equivalent.

(v) CH= Methane concentration of the dilution air in ppm carbon equivalent.

(vi) rCH4 = HC FID response to methane as measured in § 86.121(d).

(9)(i) CH= Methane emissions, in grams per test phase.

(ii) Density= Density of methane is 18.89 g/ft-carbon atom (0.6672 kg/m-carbon atom), at 68 &#xB0;F (20 &#xB0;C) and 760 mm Hg (101.3 kPa) pressure.

(10)(i) NO= Nitrous oxide emissions, in grams per test phase.

(ii) Density= Density of nitrous oxide is 51.81 g/ft(1.83 kg/m), at 68 &#xB0;F (20 &#xB0;C) and 760 mm Hg (101.3kPa) pressure.

(iii)(A) NO= Nitrous oxide concentration of the dilute exhaust sample corrected for background, in ppm.

(B) NO= NO- NO(1 - (1/DF)).

(d) For petroleum-fueled vehicles, example calculation of mass values of exhaust emissions using positive displacement pump:

(1) For the "transient" phase of the cold start test assume the following: V= 0.29344 ftrev; N = 10,485; R = 48.0 pct; R= 48.2 percent; P= 762 mm Hg; P= 22.225 mm Hg; P= 70 mm Hg; T= 570 &#xB0;R; HC= 105.8 ppm, carbon equivalent; NOx= 11.2 ppm; CO= 306.6 ppm; CO= 1.43 percent; CH= 10.74 ppm; HC= 12.1 ppm; NOx= 0.8 ppm; CO= 15.3 ppm; CO= 0.032 percent; CH= 2.20 ppm; D= 3.598 miles.

(i) V= (0.29344)(10,485)(762-70)(528)/(760)(570) = 2595.0 ftper test phase.

(ii) H = (43.478)(48.2)(22.225)/762 - (22.225)(48.2/100) = 62 grains of water per pound of dry air.

(iii) K= 1/[1 - 0.0047(62-75)] = 0.9424.

(iv) CO= [1-0.01925(1.43) - 0.000323(48)](306.6) = 293.4 ppm.

(v) CO= [1 - 0.000323(48)](15.3) = 15.1 ppm.

(vi) DF = 13.4/[1.43 + 10(105.8 + 293.4)] = 9.116.

(vii) HC= 105.8-12.1(1 - 1/9.116) = 95.03 ppm.

(viii) HC= (2595)(16.33)(95.03/1,000,000) = 4.027 grams per test phase.

(ix) NOx= 11.2 - 0.8(1 - 1/9.116) = 10.49 ppm.

(x) NOx= (2595)(54.16)(10.49/1,000,000)(0.9424) = 1.389 grams per test phase.

(xi) CO= 293.4 - 15.1(1 - 1/9.116) = 280.0 ppm.

(xii) CO= (2595)(32.97)(280/1,000,000) = 23.96 grams per test phase.

(xiii) CO= 1.43 - 0.032(1 - 1/9.116) = 1.402 percent.

(xiv) CO= (2595.0)(51.85)(1.402/100) = 1886 grams per test phase.

(xv) CH= 10.74 - 2.2 (1 - 1/9.116) = 8.78 ppm.

(xvi) NMHC= 95.03 - 8.78 = 86.25 ppm.

(xvii) NMHC= (2595)(16.33)(86.25)/1,000,000 = 3.655 grams per test phase.

(2) For the stabilized portion of the cold start test assume that similar calculations resulted in the following:

(i) HC= 0.62 gram per test phase.

(iii) CO= 5.98 grams per test phase.

(iv) CO= 2346 grams per test phase.

(3) For the "transient" portion of the hot start test assume that similar calculations resulted in the following:

(iii) CO= 5.01 grams per test phase.

(iv) CO= 1758 grams per test phase.

(4) Weighted mass emission results:

(i) HC= 0.43[(4.027 + 0.62)/(3.598 + 3.902)] + 0.57[(0.51 + 0.62)/(3.598 + 3.902)] = 0.352 gram per vehicle mile.

(ii) NOx= 0.43[(1.389 + 1.27)/(3.598 + 3.902)] + 0.57[(1.38 + 1.27)/(3.598 + 3.902)] = 0.354 gram per vehicle mile.

(iii) CO= 0.43[(23.96 + 5.98)/(3.598 + 3.902)] + 0.57[(5.01 + 5.98)/(3.598 + 3.902)] = 2.55 grams per vehicle mile.

(iv) CO= 0.43[(1886 + 2346)/(3.598 + 3.902) + 0.57[(1758 + 2346)/(3.598 + 3.902)] = 555 gram per vehicle mile.

(v) NMHC= 0.43[(3.655 + 0.50)/(3.598 + 3.902)] + 0.57[(0.44 + 0.50)/(3.598 + 3.902)] = 0.310 gram per vehicle mile.

(e) For methanol-fueled vehicles with measured fuel composition of CHO, example calculation of exhaust emissions using positive displacement pump:

(1) For the "transient" phase of the cold start test assume the following: V= 0.29344 ftrev; N = 25,801; R = 37.5 pct; Ra = 37.5 percent; P= 725.42 mm Hg; P= 22.02 mm Hg; P= 70 mm Hg; T570 deg.R; FID HC= 14.65 ppm, carbon equivalent; r = 0.788; T= 527.67 deg.R; V= 0.2818 ft; C= 7.101; AV= 15.0 ml; C= 0.256; AV= 15.0 ml; T= 527.67 deg.R; V= 1.1389 ft; C= 0.439; AV= 15.0 ml; C= 0.0; AV= 15.0 ml; C= 8.970 µg/ml; V= 5.0 ml; Q = 0.1429; T= 527.67 deg.R; V= 0.2857 ft; C= 0.39 µg/ml; V= 5.0 ml; T= 527.67 deg.R; V= 1.1043 ft; NO. = 5.273 ppm; CO= 98.8 ppm; CO= 0.469 pct; CH= 2.825 ppm; FID HC= 2.771 ppm; NO. = 0.146 ppm; CO= 1.195 ppm; CO= 0.039 percent; CH= 2.019 ppm; Dct = 3.583 miles.
