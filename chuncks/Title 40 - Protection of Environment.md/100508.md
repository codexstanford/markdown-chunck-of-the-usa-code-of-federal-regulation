(1) A motor vehicle for which the ultimate purchaser has never received the equitable or legal title is aThis kind of vehicle might commonly be thought of as "brand new" although amay include previously used parts. For example, vehicles commonly known as "glider kits," "glider vehicles," or "gliders" are new motor vehicles. Under this definition, the vehicle is new from the time it is produced until the ultimate purchaser receives the title or places it into service, whichever comes first.
