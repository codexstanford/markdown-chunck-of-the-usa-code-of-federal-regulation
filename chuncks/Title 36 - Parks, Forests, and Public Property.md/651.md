
# Title 36 - Parks, Forests, and Public Property
## Chapter I - National Park Service, Department of the Interior
### PART 7 - SPECIAL REGULATIONS, AREAS OF THE NATIONAL PARK SYSTEM
#### ยง 7.51 Curecanti National Recreation Area.
##### Personal Watercraft (PWC).

(d)PWC may operate within Curecanti National Recreation Area in the following designated areas and under the following conditions:

(1) PWC may operate and land on Blue Mesa Reservoir between Beaver Creek and Blue Mesa dam, except that PWC may not operate in the buoyed barricaded section in the vicinity of the dam.

(2) PWC must operate at "flat wake" speeds within Blue Mesa Reservoir in the following areas upstream of designated buoys:

(i) Soap Creek arm at approximate longitude 107&#xB0;8&#x2032;9&#x2033; N latitude 38&#xB0;30&#x2032;16&#x2033; W.

(ii) West Elk arm at approximate longitude 107&#xB0;16&#x2032;45&#x2033; N latitude 38&#xB0;29&#x2032;43&#x2033; W.

(iii) Cebolla arm at approximate longitude 107&#xB0;12&#x2032;16&#x2033; N latitude 38&#xB0;27&#x2032;37&#x2033; W.

(iv) Lake Fork arm at approximate longitude 107&#xB0;18&#x2032;19&#x2033; N latitude 38&#xB0;27&#x2032;2&#x2033; W.

(3) PWC must operate at "flat wake" speeds in the following areas:

(i) Within 100&#x2032; of shoreline inside Dry Creek cove.

(ii) Within 500&#x2032; of shoreline along old highway 50 and Bay of Chickens.

(iii) Within the buoyed area around Elk Creek and Lake Fork marinas.

(iv) Within the buoyed area at Iola, Stevens Creek, and Ponderosa boat launch.

(v) From Lake city bridge east to Beaver Creek.

(vi) Within 100&#x2032; of shoreline adjacent to Stevens Creek campground.

(4) PWC may only be launched from designated boat launch sites.
