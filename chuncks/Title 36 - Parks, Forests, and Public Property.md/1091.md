
# Title 36 - Parks, Forests, and Public Property
## Chapter I - National Park Service, Department of the Interior
### PART 11 - ARROWHEAD AND PARKSCAPE SYMBOLS
#### ยง 11.1 Definitions.
##### Arrowhead Symbol,

(a) The termas used in this part, refers to the insignia of the National Park Service prescribed as its official symbol by notice published in theof March 15, 1962 (27 FR 2486). That symbol, use of which had been limited by notice published in theof October 22, 1968 (33 FR 15605-06), has been reinstated as the Service's official emblem. The term "Parkscape Symbol," as used in this part, is the same insignia referred to in thenotice of October 22, 1968, as the "National Park Service Symbol." The "Parkscape Symbol" has been prescribed as the official tie tack or pin to be worn by all National Park Service uniformed employees. Moreover, the tie tack or pin may be worn by employees of the Service when not in uniform as a part of their civilian attire.
