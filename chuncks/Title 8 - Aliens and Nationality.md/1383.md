###### Factors to consider,Consideration of minimum factors:

(a)-(1)For purposes of a public charge inadmissibility determination, DHS will consider the alien's:

(i) Age;

(ii) Health, as evidenced by a report of an immigration medical examination performed by a civil surgeon or panel physician where such examination is required (to which DHS will generally defer absent evidence that such report is incomplete);

(iii) Family status, as evidenced by the alien's household size, based on the definition of household in ยง 212.21(f);

(iv) Assets, resources, and financial status, as evidenced by the alien's household's income, assets, and liabilities (excluding any income from public benefits listed in ยง 212.21(b) and income or assets from illegal activities or sources such as proceeds from illegal gambling or drug sales); and

(v) Education and skills, as evidenced by the alien's degrees, certifications, licenses, skills obtained through work experience or educational programs, and educational certificates.
