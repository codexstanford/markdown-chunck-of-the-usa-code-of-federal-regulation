
# Title 48 - Federal Acquisition Regulations System
## Chapter 6 - Department of State
### Subchapter D - Socioeconomic Programs
#### PART 619 - SMALL BUSINESS PROGRAMS
##### Subpart 619.2 - Policies
###### 619.202-70 The Department of State Mentor-Prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; Program.
####### Eligibility of prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; firms.

(h)(1) A prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; firm must be:

(i) A SB, HUBZone, SDB, WOSB, VOSB, or SDVOSB as those terms are defined in FAR 2.101;

(ii) Small in the NAICS code for the services or supplies to be provided by the prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; to the mentor; and,

(iii) Eligible for award of U.S. Government contracts.

(2) Except for SDB and HUBZone firms, a prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; firm may self-certify to a mentor firm that it meets the requirements set forth in paragraph (h)(1) of this subsection. Mentors may rely in good faith on written representations by potential prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; that they meet the specified eligibility requirements. SDB status eligibility and documentation requirements are determined by FAR 19.304. HUBZone status eligibility and documentation requirements are determined by FAR 19.1303.

(3) Prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; may have multiple mentors. prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; participating in mentor-prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; programs in addition to DOS's program should maintain a system for preparing separate reports of mentoring activity for each agency's program.
