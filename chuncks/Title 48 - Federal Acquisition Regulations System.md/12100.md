
# Title 48 - Federal Acquisition Regulations System
## Chapter 10 - Department of the Treasury
### Subchapter D - Socioeconomic Programs
#### PART 1019 - SMALL BUSINESS PROGRAMS
##### Subpart 1019.2 - Policies
###### 1019.202-70 The Treasury Mentor Prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; Program.
####### Agreement contents.

(l)The contents of the agreement will contain:

(1) Names and addresses of mentor and prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; firms and a point of contact within both firms who will oversee the agreement;

(2) Procedures for the mentor firm to notify the prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; firm, OSDBU and the Contracting Officer, in writing, at least 30 days in advance of the mentor firm's intent to voluntarily withdraw from the Mentor-Prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; Program;

(3) Procedures for a prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; firm to notify the mentor firm in writing at least 30 days in advance of the prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; firm's intent to voluntarily terminate the mentor-prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; agreement. The mentor must notify OSDBU and the Contracting Officer immediately upon receipt of such notice from the prot&#xE9;g&#xE9;;

(4) Each proposed mentor-prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; relationship must include information on the mentor's ability to provide developmental assistance to the prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; and how that assistance will potentially increase contracting and subcontracting opportunities for the prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; firm;

(5) A description of the type of developmental program that will be provided by the mentor firm to the prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; firm, to include a description of the potential subcontract work, and a schedule for providing assistance and criteria for evaluation of the prot&#xE9;g&#xE9;s developmental success;

(6) A listing of the types and dollar amounts of subcontracts that may be awarded to the prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; firm;

(7) Program participation term;

(8) Termination procedures;

(9) Plan for accomplishing work should the agreement be terminated; and

(10) Other terms and conditions, as appropriate.
