###### North Coast Recreational Yelloweye Rockfish Conservation Area.

(b)The North Coast Recreational Yelloweye Rockfish Conservation Area (YRCA) is a C-shaped area off the northern Washington coast intended to protect yelloweye rockfish. The North Coast Recreational YRCA is defined by straight lines connecting the following specific latitude and longitude coordinates in the order listed:

(1) 48&#xB0;18.00&#x2032; N. lat.; 125&#xB0;18.00&#x2032; W. long.;

(2) 48&#xB0;18.00&#x2032; N. lat.; 124&#xB0;59.00&#x2032; W. long.;

(3) 48&#xB0;11.00&#x2032; N. lat.; 124&#xB0;59.00&#x2032; W. long.;

(4) 48&#xB0;11.00&#x2032; N. lat.; 125&#xB0;11.00&#x2032; W. long.;

(5) 48&#xB0;04.00&#x2032; N. lat.; 125&#xB0;11.00&#x2032; W. long.;

(6) 48&#xB0;04.00&#x2032; N. lat.; 124&#xB0;59.00&#x2032; W. long.;

(7) 48&#xB0;00.00&#x2032; N. lat.; 124&#xB0;59.00&#x2032; W. long.;

(8) 48&#xB0;00.00&#x2032; N. lat.; 125&#xB0;18.00&#x2032; W. long.;

and connecting back to 48&#xB0;18.00&#x2032; N. lat.; 125&#xB0;18.00&#x2032; W. long.
