
# Title 50 - Wildlife and Fisheries
## Chapter I - United States Fish and Wildlife Service, Department of the Interior
### Subchapter B - Taking, Possession, Transportation, Sale, Purchase, Barter, Exportation, and Importation of Wildlife and Plants
#### PART 17 - ENDANGERED AND THREATENED WILDLIFE AND PLANTS
##### Subpart I - Interagency Cooperation
###### ยง 17.99 Critical habitat; plants on the Hawaiian Islands.
####### Kokia cookei

Molokai-Lowland Dry-Unit 1 and Molokai-Lowland Dry-Unit 2, identified in the legal descriptions in paragraph (c) of this section, constitute critical habitat foron Molokai. In units Molokai-Lowland Dry-Unit 1 and Molokai-Lowland Dry-Unit 2, the physical and biological features of critical habitat are:

(i) Elevation: Less than 3,300 ft (1,000 m).

(ii) Annual precipitation: Less than 50 in (130 cm).

(iii) Substrate: Weathered silty loams to stony clay, rocky ledges, little-weathered lava.
